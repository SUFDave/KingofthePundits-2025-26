Division 1: Davo Reclaims Top Spot After Week 7 Drama

Davo bounces back to claim first place with 15 points despite a modest 2-point week, capitalizing on Chelsea's 3-0 demolition of Nottingham Forest. His perfect home record came to an end with Bradford's 2-2 draw against Barnsley, but his 48% success rate (10/21 correct) remains formidable. Dylan surges into second place with 14 points after a brilliant 3-point haul, nailing both his home pick (Coventry 2-0 Blackburn) and away selection (Arsenal's 1-0 win at Fulham). Rob maintains third with 11 points after a single-point week, keeping his steady 40% success rate intact. Mike endured a nightmare week with zero points, missing all three predictions and seeing his success rate plummet to 29% - the lowest in Division One. The division gap has widened to 6 points between first and fourth.

Division 2: Dave K Storms Clear with Sensational 5-Point Week

In a week of seismic shifts, Dave K delivered the performance of the season with a stunning 5-point haul, rocketing from 16 to 21 points and opening up a commanding 3-point lead at the top. His draw mastery reached new heights with the Crystal Palace 3-3 Bournemouth thriller, complemented by Chelsea's away victory. Ben climbs into solo second place with 18 points after matching Dylan's 3-point week, maintaining his exceptional 52% success rate - the highest in the entire competition. His home prowess (6/7 correct) remains a fearsome weapon. Alex drops to third with 16 points despite adding a home win, while Myles sits fourth on 15 points with a solid 1-point week. Dave K's draw expertise (4/7 correct) has become the defining factor in Division Two's title race.

Week 7 Highlights

Dave K's 5-point explosion was the standout performance, featuring both an away win and that precious draw prediction. Dylan and Ben both delivered identical 3-point hauls with matching picks - Coventry's home victory and Arsenal's away win at Fulham proving to be the week's banker selections. The weekend saw Davo's remarkable six-week perfect home streak finally end, while Mike's complete blank highlighted the unpredictable nature of the competition. Both Manchester City and Chelsea featured heavily in successful predictions, with the Citizens' 2-0 home win over Everton and Chelsea's 3-0 away triumph picking up multiple points across both divisions.

The gap between divisions has narrowed - Division Two's top three (Dave K, Ben, Alex) all sit on 16+ points, while Division One's leaders are more tightly bunched between 15-9 points, suggesting tactical sophistication is slightly higher in the second tier.
