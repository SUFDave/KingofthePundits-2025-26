SEASON 2025/26

WELCOME TO KING OF THE PUNDITS

Eight skilled competitors battle across two divisions in a 30-week war of wits, where every pick counts and pride is everything. After eleven electrifying weeks, we have a HISTORIC 3-WAY TIE in Division One with Dylan, Mike, and Rob all on 21 points, while Ben has extended his Division Two lead to a commanding 31 points!

WEEK ELEVEN RESULTS

Week Eleven delivered the most dramatic twist of the entire season! Rob's sensational FULL HOUSE - the fourth of the campaign and his first personal maximum - catapults him from fourth place into a historic 3-way tie for first! The competition has never seen anything like this: three players locked together at the summit with identical points totals, while Division Two leader Ben continues his relentless march towards glory.

Rob produced the performance of the season with a perfect 6 POINTS from three correct predictions. Manchester City's thrilling 3-2 victory over Leeds secured his home pick, Bristol Rovers' 0-1 loss to Notts County delivered his away win, and the Luton 1-1 Bolton draw completed his masterpiece. His Full House rockets him from 15 points to 21 POINTS, joining the joint leaders at the summit in the most remarkable comeback of the campaign!

Dylan clings to a share of first place on 21 POINTS after managing just 1 point from Coventry's dominant 3-1 demolition of Charlton. His Crystal Palace draw prediction and Wigan away pick both missed, but his lead - once commanding - now vanishes into a three-way tie. Mike surges into the summit share with an excellent 3-point haul, combining Lincoln's 1-0 home win over Port Vale with Liverpool's comprehensive 2-0 victory at West Ham to reach 21 POINTS.

Davo slips to fourth place on 19 POINTS after Aston Villa's narrow 1-0 victory over Wolves proved his only consolation. His Chesterfield draw prediction missed as Swindon won 2-1 away, and Oxford's 2-1 home win over Ipswich denied his away pick. Just 2 points now separate all four Division One players - the tightest title race in competition history!

In Division Two, Ben continues his coronation march with another clinical 3-point performance to reach an imposing 31 POINTS. His Manchester City home pick hit the mark, Sheffield Wednesday's 2-3 defeat to Preston delivered his away win, but Liverpool's comfortable victory at West Ham denied his draw prediction. With an extraordinary 55% success rate (18/33 correct) and a 7-point lead, Ben is running away with the title!

Dave K and Alex are now locked in battle for second place on 24 POINTS apiece. Dave K endured a nightmare scoreless week as Crystal Palace fell 1-2 to Manchester United, Chelsea drew 1-1 with Arsenal (not an away win), and Sunderland beat Bournemouth 3-2 (not a draw). Alex salvaged a single point from Lincoln's 1-0 home win over Port Vale but similarly struggled with his other predictions.

Myles remains rooted to the bottom on 15 POINTS after another devastating blank week. All three of his predictions missed - Nottingham Forest lost 0-2 at home to Brighton, Coventry won 3-1 at home (not an away win at Charlton), and Millwall's 3-2 home win over Southampton was not a draw. Now 16 points adrift of Ben, Myles needs a miracle to salvage his season.

The division narratives have transformed dramatically! Division One has evolved from a one-horse race into the most thrilling three-way battle imaginable, with just 2 points covering all four players. Meanwhile, Division Two appears to be Ben's procession with a 7-point cushion that looks insurmountable. With 19 weeks remaining, the stage is set for an unforgettable finish!

THE SEASON AT A GLANCE

Welcome to the 2025/26 season of King of the Pundits! With 30 action-packed weeks ahead, our competitors are locked in a fierce battle for bragging rights and the ultimate prize.

The season lasts for 30 WEEKS, where eight pundits compete across two divisions in the ultimate test of football knowledge and prediction prowess.
The scoring system rewards bold predictions: Home Win = 1pt, Away Win = 2pts, Draw = 3pts. The rarest and most valuable outcome is correctly predicting a draw.
After eleven weeks of intense competition, we've witnessed historic moments including Rob's sensational Full House - the fourth of the competition - creating a never-before-seen 3-way tie at the Division One summit!
With £2 PER WEEK entry fees, the prize fund continues to grow. Total prize money is split equally between the Betting Fund and End of Season Prize Fund.
Current form suggests this could be the most competitive season yet! Ben's phenomenal 55% success rate leads the competition with a commanding 7-point cushion, while Division One's 3-way tie promises an unforgettable finish in the 19 weeks remaining!
