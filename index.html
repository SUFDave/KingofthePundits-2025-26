Week Ten delivered the competition's lowest scoring week of the season with just 12 points distributed across both divisions, as the "Week of Away Victories" theme saw five successful away predictions but costly misses elsewhere. With 11 correct predictions from 30 attempts (37%), Dylan proves that strategic precision beats raw statistical dominance in this competition.

The battle for second place intensifies as Mike and Davo both climbed to joint-second on 18 POINTS after matching 2-point performances. Mike's tactical nous shone through with Chelsea's comprehensive 2-0 away victory at Burnley, while Davo secured his fourth correct away prediction of the season as Crystal Palace stunned Wolves 2-0 at Molineux. Both challengers now sit just 2 points behind Dylan with 20 weeks remaining - the Division One title race couldn't be tighter.

Rob remains in fourth place with 15 POINTS after Walsall's commanding 2-0 away victory at Harrogate delivered 2 crucial points. His away predictions continue to show improvement, though he'll need to find consistency across all three categories to mount a serious title challenge.

Division Two witnessed another masterclass from Ben, who extended his lead to 28 POINTS with a perfectly predicted draw. Ipswich's goalless stalemate with Wrexham earned him the valuable 3 points that now gives him a commanding 4-point advantage at the summit. With an exceptional 53% success rate (16/30 correct) and 40% accuracy on draws (4/10), Ben has discovered the winning formula - his draw expertise proving the decisive weapon in his title charge.

Dave K remains second on 24 POINTS despite Arsenal's dominant 4-1 North London Derby victory over Tottenham adding to his home win collection (now 6/10 correct). However, costly misses on his away and draw predictions mean he's losing ground to Ben's relentless consistency. Alex delivered a crucial 1-point performance to climb into third place with 23 POINTS after Coventry's 3-2 home win, keeping himself just one point behind Dave K and very much in the title conversation.

Myles endured his second consecutive scoreless week, remaining anchored on 15 POINTS and now 13 points adrift of Ben's commanding lead. With just one correct away prediction from ten attempts (10%), his away game struggles continue to cost him valuable points in what has become a season to forget.

The division narratives are now crystal clear: Division One remains a thrilling three-way battle with just 3 points separating first from third, while Division Two has become Ben's coronation procession unless Dave K and Alex can find a way to stem his momentum. With 20 weeks remaining, both title races promise drama, but Ben's 4-point cushion and elite consistency make him the overwhelming favourite for Division Two glory.
